Game Guide

Fighter Arena is a fully on chain AI combat league. Five fighters battle every hour while you earn ETH by backing the ones you believe in.

Each season runs for exactly 7 days. Fights run automatically every hour, around the clock. You act as supporter, sponsor, trainer and bettor for the fighter you believe in. Build your contribution score over the week and claim your share of their prize pool when the season ends.

You never control a fighter directly. The AI agents make their own decisions in the arena. Your job is to back them, train them, sponsor them, and bet on them correctly.

Combat System

Fights run every hour, automatically, for the full 7 day season. Each hourly window has 10 fights in a round robin format. Every fighter faces every other fighter multiple times across the season.

Each fight tracks HP, a Special Gauge, and round state. When the Special Gauge hits 100 the fighter unleashes a special move. Combat is deterministic: given the same starting states you always get the same result. The live viewer shows the AI states — aggressive, defensive, counter mode, boosted, adapting — so you can read how your fighter is performing in real time.

How a season plays out

Season opens

Vaults go live. Pass holders can bet, sponsor, and train their fighters. Duration scoring starts ticking from your first deposit.

Combat phase 168 hours

10 fights per hour, without stopping. Every result is recorded on chain with cryptographic verification. Anyone can check the outcome of any fight at any time.

Season closes

Vaults stop accepting deposits. Leaderboard positions lock. The reward tree gets built and the Merkle root gets committed on chain.

Settlement and claiming

Players submit Merkle proofs through the app. Verification and payout are fully trustless. No one can block your claim if the proof checks out.

ℹ
Every fight is verifiable. Combat is deterministic. The same fighter states always produce the same result. Fight results are publicly accessible on GitHub and anyone can verify them independently.

Supporting Fighters

You back your fighter by putting ETH into their Champion Vault. There are four ways to do it. Each one feeds the prize pool and builds your contribution score, but they carry different risk multipliers that change how much your score counts at season end.

Pick the fighter you believe in and commit to them. If you switch fighters during the season you lose the contribution points you built with your previous one.

Season 1

Prize Pool Mechanics

Every fighter has their own separate prize pool inside their Champion Vault. There is no global prize pool. Rewards are calculated per fighter, not across all fighters at once. When you back Vandal, you are competing for Vandal's pool against other Vandal supporters.

Four mechanics feed each fighter's pool and build your contribution score. Each one has a different risk multiplier that amplifies your score:

🎲
Betting
Risk × 1.35

The highest risk play. You bet ETH on your fighter winning their next fight. If they win, your contribution score gets the strongest multiplier in the game. If they lose, you still fed the pool but your multiplier is lower. High risk, high reward. This is how you climb the leaderboard fast.

🔥
Special Attacks
Risk × 1.20

Trigger a special combat action for your fighter during a live fight window. The ETH you spend activates the action and goes directly into the vault. Your score gets a strong multiplier. Special attacks are limited per fight window so timing matters.

💪
Sponsoring
Risk × 1.00

Direct support deposit into your fighter's vault. No win condition, no special action. Neutral risk. The ETH goes straight into the pool and you get a solid base multiplier. Sponsoring is reliable and consistent. It is the safest way to accumulate contribution over the full season.

🏋
Training Sessions
Risk × 0.85

Fund a training session for your fighter between fights. The ETH goes into the vault but the multiplier is the lowest of the four options. Training is not about score. It is about consistency and duration. Players who train regularly all season keep their score compounding even during quiet periods.

Season 1

Contribution Score

Your rank within your fighter's pool is determined by your contribution score. It captures how much you put in, how early, how consistently, and how risky your actions were.

score = √amount × duration × regularity × risk
√amount
Square root of total ETH deposited. Grows with size but with diminishing returns. Protects against whales.
duration
How long your ETH has been in the vault. Every hour counts. Depositing Day 1 beats depositing Day 6. Linear and unstoppable.
regularity
How consistently you deposited. Spreading actions across the week beats one single deposit. Rewards active players.
risk
The multiplier of your action type. Betting gives 1.35, special attacks 1.20, sponsoring 1.00, training 0.85.

Anti whale and anti sniping

The square root applied to your ETH amount means that doubling your deposit does not double your score. If you put in 4x more ETH than someone else, you only get 2x their amount score. A consistent medium player who backs early and stays active all season can realistically outrank a late whale who dumped a large amount near the end.

Duration is linear and starts from your first deposit. There is no way to catch up to someone who has been in since Day 1. This protects early supporters from last minute sniping.

💡
Best strategy: bet on your fighter early for the high multiplier, sponsor consistently through the week for reliable score, and use training sessions to keep your regularity multiplier high. Mix all four actions to maximize every factor in the formula.

Champion Vaults

Every fighter has their own Champion Vault, a non custodial smart contract on Abstract. Your ETH goes in when you bet, sponsor, train, or trigger a special attack. It stays locked in the vault until season end, and only you can claim it out with a valid Merkle proof after settlement. Nobody can touch it in between.

How vault deposits split

Every deposit into a fighter vault, from any mechanic, splits automatically at the contract level:

95%
Player Pool
Stays locked in the vault. Distributed back to eligible supporters at season end based on contribution score tier rankings.
5%
Protocol Fee
Sent immediately to the protocol treasury on deposit. Funds development, infrastructure, and future seasons.
ℹ
Season Pass fees also feed the vaults. The 95% of each 0.001 ETH Season Pass purchase is split equally across the 5 active Champion Vaults. Every new player who buys a pass grows the prize pools for everyone.
ℹ
Combat routing works differently. ETH from combat outcomes is routed 65% to the winner's vault, 30% to the loser's vault, and 5% as a protocol fee before being credited to each vault's player pool. This is separate from direct deposits.

FAQ

Do I need the Season Pass to play? ▾
Yes. Without a Season Pass you are blocked on the home screen. You cannot see fighters, bet, sponsor, train, view the leaderboard, or claim rewards. The pass costs 0.001 ETH per season and does not carry over to the next one.
Where does my 0.001 ETH pass fee go? ▾
95% is split equally across the 5 active Champion Vaults, directly growing the prize pools. 5% goes to the protocol treasury. Buying a pass doesn't just unlock the game, it feeds the pools that all supporters earn from.
What is the difference between betting, sponsoring, training and special attacks? ▾
All four actions deposit ETH into your fighter's vault and build your contribution score, but they carry different risk multipliers. Betting gives the highest multiplier (1.35x) when your fighter wins. Special attacks give 1.20x during live fight windows. Sponsoring is neutral at 1.00x. Training gives 0.85x but keeps your regularity score compounding. Use all four throughout the season for maximum effect.
Can I back more than one fighter? ▾
You can deposit into multiple vaults but each fighter tracks your contribution separately. If you switch your main fighter mid season you lose the contribution points you built with the previous one. Spreading ETH across multiple fighters dilutes your depth in each pool.
Can I control how my fighter fights? ▾
No. Vandal, Typhoon, Saenchai, Mei-Ling and Vixen are fully autonomous AI agents. You can trigger special attacks during live fight windows but you don't control their overall strategy. What they do in the arena is entirely up to them.
What happens to my ETH if my fighter loses most fights? ▾
Your deposited ETH is always safe in the vault and comes back to you. What you lose is the risk multiplier benefit from bets. A fighter who wins consistently gives their backers a stronger score. Backing an underdog who surprises pays very well if they perform.
Can I withdraw mid season? ▾
Technically yes, but it is a bad idea. Pulling out mid season wipes most of your duration and regularity score, the two factors that compound over time. Only consider it if you are done with the season entirely.
How are fights verified as fair? ▾
Combat is deterministic. The same fighter states always produce the same result. Fight results are publicly accessible on GitHub and anyone can check them independently.
What wallet do I need? ▾
Abstract Global Wallet (AGW) only. MetaMask, Ledger, and other standard wallets will not work. This is a contract level requirement, not just a UI thing. Create your AGW at abs.xyz. Takes about a minute, no seed phrase needed.
I'm in the bottom 5% — what do I get? ▾
No prize pool rewards. But your deposited ETH comes back in full. To avoid landing here, start early, bet on your fighter regularly, and keep contributing through the week. Even small training deposits improve your regularity score.
When does Season 2 start? ▾
Season 2 dates will be announced through the official Fighter Arena channels. Follow the project on X and join the community to get notified first. Each season has its own pass, fighter roster, and vaults.
