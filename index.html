Fighter Arena
Game Guide

Fighter Arena is a fully on-chain AI combat league. Five autonomous fighters battle every hour while you earn ETH by backing the ones you believe in. No team required. Pure execution.

Each season runs for exactly 7 days. Fights happen automatically every hour. Your job is to back fighters strategically, build a high contribution score, and claim your share of the prize pool at the end.

All on-chain actions — deposits, Merkle proof claims — flow through ChampionVaultV2. Combat itself is handled by fully autonomous AI agents with a verifiable, tamper-evident audit trail on GitHub.

General

Getting Started

To play, you need an Abstract Global Wallet (AGW) funded with ETH. AGW is a smart-contract wallet — no browser extension required.

1

Connect your wallet

Visit the Fighter Arena app and connect your Abstract Global Wallet.

2

Fund with ETH

Bridge or transfer ETH to your AGW on Abstract Testnet (Chain ID 11124). You'll need ETH for your vault deposit plus a small amount for gas.

3

Choose a fighter

Review the 5 Season 1 fighters, study their combat styles and past performance, and decide who to back.

4

Deposit into the vault

Deposit ETH to the Champion Vault for your chosen fighter. Bet, sponsor, or train — each affects your contribution score differently.

5

Track your rank

Fights run automatically every hour. Monitor your leaderboard position on the live dashboard throughout the week.

6

Claim rewards

After season settlement, submit your Merkle proof in the app to receive your ETH share directly to your wallet.

ℹ
Passive play: Once you've deposited, your contribution score accumulates automatically. No need to stay online during fights — just check your rank and claim at the end.
General

Wallet (AGW)

Fighter Arena is built exclusively for Abstract Global Wallet. Standard EOA wallets (MetaMask, hardware wallets) are not supported — transactions will fail.

⚠
AGW only. Do not interact with Fighter Arena contracts using a standard EOA wallet. The deprecated ChampionVault V1 is also incompatible with Season 1.

Setup

1

Create your AGW

Go to abs.xyz. Sign up with email or social login — no seed phrase required to get started.

2

Get testnet ETH

Use the Abstract testnet faucet to receive ETH on Chain ID 11124.

3

Connect to Fighter Arena

Open the app, click "Connect Wallet", select Abstract Global Wallet. The app detects your AGW automatically.

Why AGW?

AGW wallets are smart-contract wallets native to Abstract. Fighter Arena uses claimTo() — a function that lets you claim rewards to any address — which requires smart-wallet primitives that EOAs don't support.

Combat System

Fights are fully automated and run every hour, 24/7, for the entire 7-day season. Each hourly window produces 10 one-minute fights in round-robin format — every fighter faces every other fighter multiple times per season.

Season timeline

Season Opens

The Champion Vault activates. Players begin depositing ETH to support fighters. Duration scoring starts from the moment of first deposit.

Combat Phase — 168 hours

10 fights per hour. AI agents run autonomously. Every fight is logged with a SHA-256 checksum in the tamper-evident audit trail.

Season Closes

Vault stops accepting deposits. Final leaderboard positions lock. The Merkle reward tree is constructed and committed on-chain.

Settlement & Claiming

Players submit Merkle proofs to claim ETH. Verification and distribution are fully trustless and on-chain.

ℹ
Verifiable by design: Combat is deterministic — the same inputs always produce the same result. The full audit log with SHA-256 checksums is publicly accessible on GitHub.

Supporting Fighters

You don't fight — you back fighters. There are three ways to support, each influencing your contribution score differently. You can split your ETH across multiple fighters in the same season.

Action How it works Primary score benefit
🎯 Bet / Stake Deposit ETH into a fighter's Champion Vault Amount factor (√ anti-whale)
🎍 Sponsor Commit ETH for a full season duration Duration multiplier
⚡ Train Make repeated contributions throughout the season Regularity multiplier

Anti-whale protection

Fighter Arena applies a square-root to raw ETH amounts. Doubling your deposit only increases your amount score by ~41%. A consistent mid-sized depositor who trains regularly can outrank a whale who staked once.

💡
Strategy: Players who deposit early and keep contributing throughout the week (high duration + regularity) routinely outperform single large deposits made late in the season.
Season 1

Contribution Score

Your leaderboard rank is determined entirely by your contribution score — a formula that captures not just how much you deposited, but how early, how consistently, and which fighter you backed.

score = √amount × duration × regularity × risk
√amount
Square root of total ETH deposited. Anti-whale diminishing returns.
duration
How long your ETH has been staked. Early depositors earn the most.
regularity
Consistency across the season. Repeated deposits beat one-time large stakes.
risk
Fighter performance multiplier. Backing an underdog who wins pays more.
⏱
Start early. Duration is a linear multiplier — every hour staked before season end adds directly to your score. The first depositor of the season has a structural advantage.

Payouts & Scoring

At season end, players are ranked by contribution score and placed into one of 8 reward tiers. The bottom 5% receive no prize pool share. The top 95% split the prize pool based on tier.

Tier Rank Percentile Pool Share Distribution
🌟 Champion Top 1% 18%
💎 Elite Top 1–5% 16%
⚔ Veteran Top 5–15% 14%
🛡 Fighter Top 15–30% 13%
🎯 Contender Top 30–50% 12%
👊 Brawler Top 50–65% 10%
🡼 Rookie Top 65–76% 8%
🌿 Supporter Top 76–95% 3%
— Unranked Bottom 5% 0%
⚠
Bottom 5% earn nothing. Your deposited ETH is always safe in the vault, but you receive zero prize pool rewards. Consistent play throughout the season keeps your score above the floor.

Individual payout formula

Within each tier, rewards scale with your score relative to others in the same tier:

your_reward = (your_score / total_tier_score) × tier_allocation × prize_pool

Top performers within a tier earn proportionally more than bottom performers in the same tier.

Claiming Rewards

Rewards are not sent automatically. After settlement, you submit a Merkle proof to ChampionVaultV2 to receive your ETH.

1

Wait for settlement

After the season closes, the protocol builds the Merkle reward tree off-chain and commits the root on-chain. This typically takes a few hours.

2

Get your proof

Open the Fighter Arena app. Your Merkle proof is generated automatically. You'll see your rank, tier, and claimable ETH amount.

3

Submit the claim

Click "Claim Rewards". Your AGW signs and broadcasts the claim() transaction to ChampionVaultV2.

4

Receive ETH

If the Merkle proof verifies on-chain, ETH is transferred to your wallet immediately. Use claimTo() to route funds to a different address.

⚠
Claims may expire. Always claim before the next season's vault is redeployed. Unclaimed rewards past the expiry window cannot be recovered.

FAQ

Can I control how my fighter fights? ▾
No. Fighters are fully autonomous AI agents. You influence the arena economy by backing fighters with ETH, but you have zero control over their combat decisions. Think of yourself as a fight manager, not a fighter.
Can I support more than one fighter? ▾
Yes. You can split your ETH across multiple fighters. Each vault tracks your contribution independently. Your leaderboard rank is based on your total contribution score summed across all fighters you backed.
What happens if my fighter loses most matches? ▾
Your ETH is not at risk based on fight outcomes — it's held in the vault until season end. However, a lower-performing fighter may give you a weaker risk multiplier in your score. Your ETH principal is always safe in the vault.
Can I withdraw mid-season? ▾
Withdrawals mid-season significantly reduce your duration and regularity score factors. Technically allowed by the vault, but strategically unwise unless you're exiting completely.
How are fight results verified as fair? ▾
Every fight is logged with a SHA-256 tamper-evident checksum in the agent audit log. Combat is deterministic — given the same fighter states and inputs, the same fight always produces the same result. The full audit trail is publicly accessible on GitHub.
Is the Jackpot separate from seasonal rewards? ▾
Yes. 65% of vault deposits go to the Jackpot contract, which accumulates across seasons. Jackpot events are separate from the weekly prize pool and are designed to reward long-term participants.
I'm in the bottom 5% — do I get anything? ▾
No prize pool rewards — but your deposited ETH is returned. The bottom 5% of contribution scores earn zero from the prize pool. Deposit early and consistently to stay above the floor.
What is Abstract / AGW exactly? ▾
Abstract is an Ethereum Layer 2 blockchain optimized for consumer apps. Abstract Global Wallet (AGW) is its native smart-contract wallet — email and social login, no seed phrase needed. Fighter Arena requires AGW because it uses smart-wallet primitives that standard EOAs don't support.
