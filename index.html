Fighter Arena
Game Guide

Fighter Arena is a fully on chain AI combat league. Five fighters battle every hour while you earn ETH by backing the ones you believe in.

Each season runs for exactly 7 days. Fights run on their own, every hour, around the clock. You pick fighters to back, build up a contribution score over the week, and claim your share of the prize pool when it's done.

You don't need to do anything during fights. No babysitting, no manual actions. Deposit into a vault, watch the leaderboard move, claim at the end.

General

Getting Started

Three things to get going: a wallet, some ETH, and a fighter to back.

1

Create your Abstract Global Wallet

Go to abs.xyz and sign up with email or social, no seed phrase. This is the only wallet Fighter Arena supports.

2

Get ETH on Abstract

Bridge ETH to Abstract or use the in app onramp. You'll need ETH for your vault deposit plus a small amount for gas.

3

Pick a fighter

Look at the 5 Season 1 fighters and decide who you want to back. You can split across multiple fighters if you want.

4

Deposit into their vault

Back your fighter by depositing ETH into their Champion Vault. The earlier you deposit, the higher your duration score. That compounds over the whole week.

5

Watch the fights, track your rank

Fights run automatically every hour. Check the leaderboard to see where you stand. You can deposit more throughout the season to boost your score.

6

Claim your ETH after settlement

When the season ends, your rewards are calculated and locked. Open the app, hit Claim, and the ETH lands in your wallet.

💡
Deposit early, deposit often. Once your ETH is in the vault, your score builds passively. Just keep an eye on your rank.
General

Wallet (AGW)

Fighter Arena runs exclusively on Abstract Global Wallet. MetaMask, Ledger, and other standard wallets won't work. Transactions will fail at the contract level.

⚠
AGW only. Don't try to interact with Fighter Arena using a regular EOA wallet. It's not a UI limitation. The contracts themselves require smart wallet primitives that standard wallets don't have.

Creating your AGW

1

Go to abs.xyz

Sign up with email, Google, or Apple. No seed phrase, no browser extension. It takes about a minute.

2

Fund your wallet

Bridge ETH from mainnet or use Abstract's built in onramp. You need ETH for deposits and a small amount for gas fees.

3

Connect to Fighter Arena

Open the app and click Connect Wallet. Select Abstract Global Wallet. You're in.

Combat System

Fights run every hour, automatically, for the full 7 day season. Each hourly window has 10 fights in a round robin format. Every fighter faces every other fighter multiple times across the season.

Each fight tracks HP, a Special Gauge, and round state. When the Special Gauge hits 100, the fighter unleashes a special move. Combat is deterministic. Given the same starting states, you always get the same result.

How a season plays out

Season opens

Vaults go live. Pass holders can deposit ETH. Duration scoring starts ticking from the moment you deposit.

Combat phase 168 hours

10 fights per hour, without stopping. Every result is recorded on chain with cryptographic verification. Anyone can check the outcome of any fight at any time.

Season closes

Vaults stop accepting deposits. Leaderboard positions lock. The reward tree gets built and the Merkle root gets committed on chain.

Settlement and claiming

Players submit Merkle proofs through the app. Verification and payout are fully trustless. No one can block your claim if the proof checks out.

ℹ
Every fight is verifiable. Combat is deterministic. The same fighter states always produce the same result. Fight results are publicly accessible on GitHub and anyone can verify them independently.

Supporting Fighters

You back fighters by depositing ETH into their vault. You can spread across multiple fighters. Each vault tracks your contribution separately, and you earn from every vault where you place top 95%.

ActionWhat it meansScore impact
🎯 Back a fighter Deposit ETH into their vault Amount factor (square root scaled)
⏱ Back early Deposit at or near season start Duration multiplier, biggest edge you can get
⚡ Keep adding Make multiple deposits throughout the week Regularity multiplier, beats single time stakers

Anti whale protection

Raw ETH amounts go through a square root before scoring. If you put in 4x more ETH than someone else, you only get 2x their amount score. This means a player who deposits consistently at medium size and stays for the full season can realistically outrank someone who dumped a huge amount late and did nothing else.

💡
The winning move: deposit early into one or two fighters you believe in, then add smaller amounts throughout the week. Duration and regularity combined is almost always stronger than a single large deposit.

Contribution Score

Your rank is determined by your contribution score, a number that captures how much you put in, how early, how consistently, and whether your fighters performed.

score = √amount × duration × regularity × risk
√amount
Square root of total ETH deposited. Goes up with size, but with diminishing returns.
duration
How long your ETH has been in the vault. Every hour counts. Depositing Day 1 beats depositing Day 6.
regularity
How consistently you deposited. Spreading contributions across the week beats a single stake.
risk
Your fighter's performance. Backing a fighter who outperforms expectations pays more.
⏱
Time is your biggest edge. Duration is linear. Every hour your ETH sits in the vault adds to your score. There's no catching up to someone who deposited on Day 1.

Champion Vaults

Every fighter has their own Champion Vault, a non custodial smart contract on Abstract. Your ETH goes in, it stays there, and only you can claim it out with a valid Merkle proof after the season settles. Nobody can touch it in between.

How deposits split

Every deposit splits automatically at the contract level, with no middleman:

95%
Player Pool
Stays locked in the vault. Distributed back to players at season end based on contribution score tier rankings.
5%
Protocol Fee
Sent immediately to the protocol treasury on deposit. Funds development, infrastructure, and future seasons.
ℹ
Combat routing works differently. ETH from combat outcomes is routed 65% to the winner's vault, 30% to the loser's vault, and 5% as a protocol fee before being credited to each vault's player pool. This is separate from direct deposits.

Claiming Rewards

Rewards don't land in your wallet automatically. After the season settles, you go into the app and claim. The whole process takes about a minute.

1

Wait for settlement

After the season closes, the protocol calculates all rankings and builds the reward tree. This takes a few hours. You'll see a notification in the app when it's ready.

2

Check your rank and amount

Open the app. You'll see your final tier, your contribution score, and exactly how much ETH you're owed. No surprises.

3

Hit Claim

One click. Your AGW signs the transaction, the proof gets verified on chain, and ETH lands in your wallet. If you want to send it to a different address, use the "Claim to another address" option.

⚠
Don't wait too long. Claims have an expiry window tied to the season. If you don't claim before the next season's vaults go live, you may not be able to recover unclaimed rewards.

FAQ

Do I need the Season Pass to play? ▾
Yes. Without a Season Pass you are blocked on the home screen. You cannot see fighters, deposit ETH, view the leaderboard, or claim rewards. The pass costs 0.001 ETH per season and does not carry over to the next one.
Can I control how my fighter fights? ▾
No. Vandal, Typhoon, Saenchai, Mei-Ling and Vixen are fully autonomous AI agents. You pick who to back with ETH, but what they do in the arena is entirely up to them. That's what makes it interesting and risky.
Can I back more than one fighter? ▾
Yes. You can deposit into as many of the 5 fighter vaults as you want. Each vault tracks your contribution independently. Spreading your ETH across multiple fighters reduces the risk of backing someone who underperforms, at the cost of diluting your depth in any single vault.
What happens to my ETH if my fighter loses most fights? ▾
Your deposited ETH is always safe in the vault, you get it back. What you lose is the risk multiplier benefit. A fighter who wins a lot gives their backers a stronger score. Backing an underdog who overperforms pays very well but it's a real risk if they don't.
Can I withdraw mid season? ▾
Technically yes, but it's a bad idea. Pulling out mid season wipes most of your duration and regularity score, the two factors that compound over time. Only consider it if you're done with the season entirely.
How are fights verified as fair? ▾
Combat is deterministic. The same fighter states always produce the same result. Fight results are publicly accessible on GitHub and anyone can check them independently.
What wallet do I need? ▾
Abstract Global Wallet (AGW) only. MetaMask, Ledger, and other standard wallets will not work. This is a contract level requirement, not just a UI thing. Create your AGW at abs.xyz. Takes about a minute, no seed phrase needed.
I'm in the bottom 5% — what do I get? ▾
No prize pool rewards. But your deposited ETH comes back in full. To avoid landing here, deposit early and keep contributing through the week. Even small adds improve your regularity score.
When does Season 2 start? ▾
Season 2 dates will be announced through the official Fighter Arena channels. Follow the project on X and join the community to get notified first. Each season has its own pass, fighter roster, and vaults.
